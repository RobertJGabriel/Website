Patents

A Computer System

This invention relates to a computer system and a method of operating the computer system. The system and method of the present invention are particularly suited to an environment such as a classroom environment or other environment where it is desirable to monitor and/or control the use of one or more computers.
The use of computers in schools is becoming more widespread. Many schools in developed countries have a dedicated computer room where there are provided a number of computers for use by the student body. In many cases, computer classes are incorporated into the curriculum to develop the computer literacy skills of the students.
Some commentators believe that, over time, the use of computers will replace the use of textbooks in the classroom. As the cost of computers fall, this is becoming closer to reality. It is envisaged by some that in the not-too-distant future, there will be a computer provided at each student’s station for use by that student during the lesson.
There are however a number of problems associated with the use of computers in the classroom. For example, often, the teacher is not in a position to view the content on the computer screen that the student is looking at or working on at a particular moment in time. It is quite possible that the student may be viewing content or engaged in an activity that is unrelated to the lesson or even contrary to school policy. Therefore, it is important to closely monitor and control the use of the computers. Secondly, heretofore there has not been provided a system or method that directly encourages or quantifies appropriate behaviour and discourages or quantifies inappropriate behaviour by the students.
It is an object of the present invention to provide a system and method that overcome at least some of the problems with the known systems and methods.
